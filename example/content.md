# 6.1 Angles: The Art of the Turn

### 📐 What Is an Angle
- An angle is formed when two straight lines meet.
- The common endpoint is called the **vertex**.
- Angles describe how much one line turns away from another.

### 🧭 How to Measure an Angle
[flow]
1. Align the center - Place the protractor's center exactly on the vertex.
2. Set the baseline - Line up the zero mark with one arm of the angle.
3. Read the scale - Find where the second arm meets the degree markings.
[/flow]

### 🎯 Important Angle Targets
- **45°** - an acute angle with a sharp turn
- **90°** - a right angle, like the corner of a square
- **120°** - an obtuse angle with a wider opening

> **Check the size before reading the number**
> If the angle looks smaller than a right angle, it should be less than 90°.
>
> If it opens wider than a right angle, choose the scale that gives a value greater than 90°.

# 6.2 Regular Polygons: Perfect Symmetry

### 🔷 What Makes a Polygon Regular
- A regular polygon is a 2D shape with all sides equal.
- Its interior angles are also equal.
- Regular polygons are known for their symmetry and balanced appearance.

### 👥 Polygon Family from 3 to 8 Sides
| Sides | Name | Everyday Clue |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 3 | Equilateral Triangle | Three equal edges |
| 4 | Square | Four equal sides and right angles |
| 5 | Regular Pentagon | Five equal sides |
| 6 | Regular Hexagon | Honeycomb pattern |
| 7 | Regular Heptagon | Seven equal sides |
| 8 | Regular Octagon | Stop sign shape |

### ✏️ Tools for Accurate Drawing
- **Square grids** help when drawing shapes with right angles.
- **Isometric grids** help show shapes from different viewpoints.
- **Computer software** makes precise geometric construction easier.

# 6.2.2 Interior Angles: Measuring the Inside Turn

### 🧮 Formula for Interior Angles
- Use this formula to find the interior angle of a regular polygon:

```prompt [label="Interior angle formula"]
(n - 2) x 180° / n
```

- **n** represents the number of sides.

### 📋 Interior Angle Cheat Sheet
- Triangle: **60°**
- Square: **90°**
- Pentagon: **108°**
- Hexagon: **120°**
- Octagon: **135°**

> **The number of sides changes the angle pattern**
> As the number of sides increases, each interior angle becomes larger and the shape looks closer to a circle.

# 6.3 Problem Solving: Space in the Real World

### 🕵️ Angle Facts to Remember
- Angles on a straight line add up to **180°**.
- Angles around a point add up to **360°**.
- These facts help us find missing angles in diagrams.

### 📏 Perimeter and Area
- **Perimeter** is the total distance around a shape.
- **Area** is the amount of space inside a shape.
- For regular polygons, area can sometimes be found by splitting the shape into smaller triangles.

### 📦 Volume of 3D Shapes
- Volume measures how much space a solid shape occupies.
- For cubes and cuboids, multiply:

```prompt [label="Volume formula"]
Length x Width x Height
```

- Example: A box with length **5 cm**, width **2 cm**, and height **10 cm** has a volume of **100 cm^3**.
